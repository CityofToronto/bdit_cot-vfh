Private Tranpsportation Companies (PTC) trips have grown rapidly since the service was first licensed by the City in September 2016. Understanding these new services and their impact is key to developing policies that support the benefits of these services while minimizing adverse effects on traffic, the environment and the equity of mobility services.

PTC trips have grown by 180% in 2.5 years

PTC trips have grown rapidly since the service was first licensed by the City in September 2016, increasing to 176,000 daily trips in March 2019 – an increase of over 180%. Most of these trips were made in the district of Toronto and East York, which in September 2018 accounted for 61% of the total number of daily trips, followed by North York (16%), Etobicoke York (13), and Scarborough (9%).

PTC trips are concentrated downtown

PTC trips are concentrated downtown throughout the day. The largest concentration occurs in the evening after 19:00 (post-PM peak) where in the Waterfront Communities-The Island neighbourhood, bordered by Bathurst St, Queen St/Front St, the Don River, and Lake Ontario, trips account for about 14% of traffic.

Select a ward from the drop-down menu and choose to display data for Pick-ups & Drop-offs, or each separately, from the Trip Type menu. Pick-ups and drop-offs at the same locations are counted and displayed on the map as proportionally-sized circles (only clusters > 14 are shown). When zooming out, nearby circles automatically cluster together into larger circles. If you have selected Pick-ups & Drop-offs, the map circles are coloured according to the fraction of trips that are pick-ups: orange for pick-ups only, purple for drop-offs only, and shades in between for decreasing proportions.
