Key findings and implications

Private Tranpsportation Companies (PTC) trips have grown rapidly since the service was first licensed by the City in September 2016. Understanding these new services and their impact is key to developing policies that support the benefits of these services while minimizing adverse effects on traffic, the environment and the equity of mobility services. In response to the City Council Recommendation 92, Item LS10.3, “A New Vehicle-for-Hire Bylaw to Regulate Toronto's Ground Transportation Industry”, adopted by City Council on May 3, 2016, the Transportation Impacts of Vehicle-for-Hire in the City of Toronto report was released in June 2019 and looks at what is most likely the first wave of disruptions from these new mobility-as-a-service businesses.

PTC trips have grown by 180% in 2.5 years

PTC trips have grown rapidly since the service was first licensed by the City in September 2016, increasing to 176,000 daily trips in March 2019 – an increase of over 180%. Most of these trips were made in the district of Toronto and East York, which in September 2018 accounted for 61% of the total number of daily trips, followed by North York (16%), Etobicoke York (13), and Scarborough (9%).

PTC trips are concentrated downtown

PTC trips are concentrated downtown throughout the day. The largest concentration occurs in the evening after 19:00 (post-PM peak) where trips account for about 14% of traffic in the Waterfront Communities-The Island neighbourhood, bordered by Bathurst St, Queen St/Front St, the Don River, and Lake Ontario.

Shared trips: matched mostly downtown

Ride sharing is a feature that can be requested when booking a trip with for example Lyft Line and Uber Pool. These services match passengers with others heading on similar paths. Shared trips are 45% more likely to be requested outside the core. However, 52% of the 42,288 daily shared requests in September 2018 were successfully matched, or 22,076 trips/day.

The map below shows the percentage of shared trips requested by neighbourhood in September 2018, with the largest concentration in the suburbs, especially in Etobicoke North. Compare with the actual percentage of shared trip requests that were matched by selecting from the drop-down menu. Although shared trips were mostly requested outside downtown and centrals areas, a smaller percentage of trips in outside areas were matched compared with central neighbourhoods.

PTC trips have clear Time-of-Day patterns

The number of trips per hour over the course of an average week in September 2018 in the City reveals distinctive patterns, as shown in the line chart below: during weekdays, trips peak during morning and evening periods, suggesting that PTCs are used for commuting. PTC trips are considerably higher on Friday and Saturday evenings, likely to accommodate entertainment-related activities.
